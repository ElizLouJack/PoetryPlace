Poems From Home
Boxes

Why, why, why do we have boxes.
The cuboidal, cylindrical or cubic shapes with or without lids.
Years and tears spill from the open crevices letting out memories long forgotten or to close to the brim.
Heartbreak, hate, love cascade like waterfalls spilling out.
Thundering a tumultuous tale of all our lives.
Childhood, adolescence, adulthood live in numbered cardboard some tightly lidded,
unable to open some pain some love the best and worst sealed sometimes for eternity.
Some boxes just contain words.
Sad, terrible words or amazing beautiful words.
The ones used to describe me or perhaps to hide me.
My castle, my prison cell, my home my hell.
The boxes are sturdy, inside them its hell.
Words like weirdo and bitch, loopy and snitch,
Spoken by others never forgotten.
Words like smart and crazy, sad and lazy,
Sporty and broken, lonely and no one.
These boxes that define me are sad crazy.
I am not willing to be defined as man or women or even non-binary.
No pretty little boxes should have ever defined me.
Because each way I describe me never encompasses me.
Each box I am plopped in is like trying to find an alien clothes,
Who has 10 heads, 9 arms,8 legs and100 toes.You/'ll never come close.
So why why why do we have boxes.

Hogwash, A Famous Poem by Anon

Twas a fine October morning,
one September, last July!
The moon lay thick upon the ground,
the mud shone in the sky!

The flowers sang so sweetly,
the birds were in full bloom!
As I hurried down the cellar steps,
to sweep the upstairs room!

The time was Tuesday morning,
on Wednesday, just at night!
I saw ten-thousand miles away,
a house, just out of sight!

Its doors projected backwards
. The front was at the back!
It stood alone, between two more,
and it was whitewashed black!
